01  Intro

Why AI energy
demand matters

The energy required for AI is already a non-trivial part of our lives. AI queries consume a growing share of global data center power — translating directly into carbon emissions and pressure on energy infrastructure globally.

Raw energy data (Wh per 1,000 queries) comes from the HuggingFace AIEnergyScore Leaderboard, measured on NVIDIA H100 GPUs via CodeCarbon. CO₂ is estimated by multiplying Wh by the IEA 2023 global average carbon intensity.

Energy Consumption:
Powering the Giants

Top 10 highest energy consumption AI models per 1,000 queries. Reasoning-mode models dominate entirely — EXAONE 4.0 32B leads at 18.99 Wh/1k queries.

Top 10 highest energy consumption Developer · total Wh per 1,000 queries

The bar chart zooms into individual models, where all top 10 are Reasoning-type: EXAONE 4.0 32B consumes nearly double the energy of the second-place SmolLM3 3B, confirming that chain-of-thought reasoning is the single biggest driver of AI energy cost. Some AI models can be set to "think" at different depths before giving an answer. This internal thinking process is called chain-of-thought reasoning, and the amount of thinking allowed is called the thinking budget. Low — the model thinks briefly before answering. Least energy used. Medium — the model thinks more carefully. Moderate energy. High — the model thinks extensively, checking and verifying its logic before responding. Most energy used.

Did you know how long
1,00 AI queries could
power your LED?

One way to make Wh tangible: a 10W LED bulb. Dividing energy by 10W gives seconds of continuous light equivalent to that AI workload.

LED equivalent formula
Wh ÷ 10W × 3,600 = seconds
EXAONE 4.0 32B / 1,000 queries → 18.99 Wh → 6,837 seconds ≈ 1.9 hours of a 10W LED

The Invisible Link:
From Math to Emissions

Every time you ask an AI to write an email or debug code, it performs with real infrastructure. These calculations require physical electricity to power the processors. The relationship is simple: Energy is the input, and CO₂ is the consequence.

Reasoning models carry a heavier environmental price tag because they think "longer" — generating extended chain-of-thought token sequences that can consume 150–700× more energy than standard inference on identical hardware.

CO₂ estimation formula
Wh × 0.417 g/Wh = CO₂ (grams)
IEA 2023 global average grid carbon intensity · varies ≈35× by region (Norway ≈0.012 vs Poland ≈0.70 g/Wh)
This is an estimation — not a directly measured value.
